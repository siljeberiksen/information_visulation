Project idea


Why "green" practices?

In the Global Risks Report for 2023, the first four positions are occupied by risks related to the environment
[World Economic Forum, 2023].

Focus of programs aimed at achieving sustainable development goals in the field of ecology
is the formation of eco-practices among the population [United Nations, 2023].

Total number of publications on the topic of environmental practices from different scientific fields
‍increases markedly every year [based on calculations made by the authors over the past 10 years in Scopus
as of the beginning of 2024].
‍

It can be assumed that new connections are emerging between the research subjects and the study conditions.‍
‍
‍
‍

What do we want to explore?

Question 1. What are the conceptual trends in environmental practices research for 2013–2022?
‍
Question 2. What are the methodological trends in environmental practices research for 2013–2022?
‍
Question 3. Is there the relationship between the types of publications on environmental practices
and the level of academic reputation in the various journals that publish them for 2013–2022?
‍
Question 4. Is there the relationship between the types of publications on environmental practices
and the country of the author's affiliation for 2013–2022?

Two main areas of study: behavioral and government

We can see two clusters:
1. Environmental practices in the field of politics, state and government
2. Variations of environmental behavior and knowledge about it, as well as a focus on the theory of planned behavior

Methodological articles are more often published
in highly rated (Q1-Q2) journals

Authors from most countries focus specifically
on the conceptual study of "green" practices

Of all the analyzed countries of affilations (N = 66), only in 9 countries the authors:
‍
- focus on methodological aspects (Latvia, Peru)
‍
OR
‍
- try to make theoretical and methodological contributions
(New Zeland, Nepal, Japan, Egypt, Serbia, UAE and Ethiopia)
